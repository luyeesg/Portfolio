About State of Mac and its goal

17 Mar. 22

How did we get here?

Back in 2020, Apple surprised the world in the WWDC by announcing the transition away from using Intel in favor of Apple silicon. At the end of that same year, Apple launched their new ARM-based chips that shocked everyone due to the power of the Apple silicon. The M1 chip was faster, more power-efficient, and had better battery life for their laptops than Intel chips.

Although all these things weren't only benefits. Due to the incompatibility between ARM-based chips and the Intel x86 architecture Apple had to dropped support for Windows Boot Camp which was the main way to run games on Mac. Adding that Apple also dropped support for 32-bits apps so from one day to another hundreds of games couldn't run on Mac things weren't looking good for gaming on Apple Silicon.

These last two years the community has been trying to improve the gaming experience on Mac by creating compatibility layers like Crossover, emulators, and virtual machines so this is where State of Mac comes in.

So what is State of Mac?

State of Mac is the easiest way to know if your favorite game runs on Apple silicon and find the best way to run that game. Also, you can collaborate with State of Mac by adding your own experience running a game on Apple silicon.

The goal of State of Mac

Our goal with State of Mac is to give the most accurate reports about the performance of games on Apple silicon and become the biggest Mac gaming community in the world.

We'll try to achieve this goal by making State of Mac the most convenient way to be awarded about the state of gaming on Mac, everything from the UI & UX to the tools provided of the site will be so expedient for the user that will not be a reason to use another alternative.
